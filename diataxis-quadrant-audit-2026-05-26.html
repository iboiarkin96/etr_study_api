Diátaxis quadrant audit — 166 internal pages, 100-pt rubric

Per-page audit of every page in services/portal/internal/ that declares a Diátaxis quadrant (data-page-type ∈ {tutorial, how-to, reference, explanation}). Each page is scored on a 100-point rubric covering quadrant fit, required structure, cross-page design uniformity, anti-pattern absence, and cross-quadrant linking. Reference is weighted more heavily on design uniformity because content uniformity across Reference sub-types (API spec, checklist, data-table, glossary, matrix) is not the goal.

Key findings (TL;DR)

  • Design chrome is universally uniform. 100% of the 166 audited pages link to runtime/internal/entry.css, mount aside[data-component=sidebar] with the same nav-tree-internal.json, carry a topbar__breadcrumbs, render main.container > article.docs-prose > header.page-head, and expose a #docs-top-nav mount point. Theme toggle, font preconnect, and entry.js script tag are uniform.
  • Quadrant fit is high across the board. Tutorials, How-to, and Explanation pages overwhelmingly match their declared quadrant by voice and structure. Only one true mis-classification was found (see § 7).
  • See-also blocks violate feedback_no_see_also in 11 files. 1 under how-to/service/local-development.html and 10 under foundations/reference/sa/ (discoverability, voice, principles, topology, visual-contract, process, style-guide, catalog, document-types, page-skeleton).
  • Footer history is missing on 13 stubs. 8 redirect stubs in reference/templates/, 3 redirect stubs in reference/qa/templates/, and 2 in how-to/incidents/{postmortems/_template,runbooks/0000-template}.html. These are by-design forwards — keep them lightweight, but add a one-line history row for ownership trace.
  • 11 Explanation-shaped pages live under foundations/reference/sa/. They correctly self-declare data-page-type="explanation", so the page itself is fine — but the folder name reference/ implies the Reference quadrant. Either rename the folder semantics («SA library, mixed quadrants») in the topology page, or move the explanation-shaped pages to foundations/explanation/sa/.
  • «Next step» card missing on 12 of 17 tutorials. Template requires every tutorial to end with a Next step link to a sibling How-to / Reference / sibling tutorial. Most tutorials jump straight from final phase to history footer.
  • Explicit Verify block missing on 11 of 29 How-to pages. Template defines Verify as non-negotiable. Several QA and Service how-tos rely on implicit verification ("checklist passes") rather than a runnable success criterion (exit code, HTTP status, CI gate).
  • 3 How-to titles are not verb-first. service/local-development.html, service/make-commands-and-workflows.html, docs/docs-pipeline.html use noun-phrase titles. The page bodies are how-to-shaped; just the title violates the rule.
  • 2 template pages mis-tagged as how-to. foundations/reference/sa/templates/{runbook,postmortem}.html self-declare as how-to. Templates describe a form, not perform a task — should be reference like their 15 siblings.

Scoring rubric

Each page is scored across 5 (or 6 for Reference) sub-scales, in 5-point increments. Subscale weights vary by quadrant — Reference gets the heaviest design-uniformity weight because content uniformity across its sub-types (API spec / checklist / matrix / data-table / glossary) is not expected.

Tutorials · common defects & strengths

  • Missing or underspecified «Next step» card — 12 of 17 body tutorials end at the history footer instead of routing the reader to a sibling How-to / Reference / next Tutorial. Template requires this.
  • «You're done» lacks loop recap — 8 of 17 have bare checklists; template requires the «That is the X loop — A → B → C» sentence.
  • Optional / branching paths in main body — 3 tutorials (accessibility audit, qa/onboarding, regression slice) put conditionals in the main spine instead of in ghost-cards.
  • Checkpoint pattern — 17 of 17 body tutorials end every non-final phase with «✓ Check your progress».
  • 3-verb lede — 16 of 17 use the canonical «Verb X, verb Y, verb Z. The point is to feel the loop — not to…» formula.
  • Real-artefact deliverables — every tutorial ends in a file/test/report, not a feeling.
  • One outlier: tutorials/qa/onboarding.html uses Day-N structure instead of Phase-N — breaks sidebar consistency.

§ 9 · Methodology & sources

The audit was conducted on 2026-05-26 by re-reading each of the 166 pages (or partial reads — first ~200 lines + targeted grep — for the larger Reference operation specs) against the canonical templates in foundations/reference/sa/templates/ and the rule page foundations/reference/sa/diataxis.html. Scoring used 5-point increments per sub-scale. Design-uniformity findings were cross-checked via repo-wide grep over each subtree (entry.css presence, sidebar mount, breadcrumbs, page-head, docs-history footer, See-also/Sources block absence).

The audit re-scored 13 pages in foundations/reference/sa/ by their declared data-page-type rather than by the folder name they live in. An earlier scoring pass mechanically dropped these to 0 on the Reference rubric — that was overzealous, since the pages are correctly tagged for their own content; the tension is folder-vs-quadrant, not content-vs-declaration.

Out of scope: the ~290 non-quadrant pages in internal/ (practice pages under team/roles/*/practices/, ADRs, RFCs, audits, service descriptors, code reference, person profiles). These have their own templates and rubrics.
