How the four trees relate

01

ADR records the choice

Every architectural commitment lands as a numbered ADR with a permanent file. The number is the stable link across reorgs, refactors, and renames.
02

RFC expands the design

When the implementation needs space — algorithms, schemas, policies — the RFC carries the detail an ADR shouldn't.
03

Audit grades the state

Dated reports score the system against an external rubric and surface gaps the team turns into backlog items.
04

Backlog tracks the work

Findings, RFC follow-ups, and product asks line up here with status and priority. Closes the loop from decided back to delivered.
