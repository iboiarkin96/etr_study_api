Overview

Assessment type: Systems Analysis Maturity — 25 practices in 5 groups, world-class lens (Google · Stripe · GitLab · Spotify · Netflix · Backstage).
Formation date: 13 May 2026.
Canonical practice catalog: analysis/practices.html (radar AS-IS / TO-BE).
Companion assessments: Tech Docs Maturity v2 (10 May 2026) — 144 docs-ops practices; Project Maturity (10 May 2026) — 30 engineering/SRE/security practices.

What this assessment adds vs the two prior ones. Tech Docs v2 measures DocOps surface (information architecture, templates, automation). Project Maturity measures engineering / SRE / security. Neither covers the full Systems-Analysis artefact ladder a Senior SA at Google would expect: Design Docs, DDD bounded contexts, stakeholder maps, risk register, SLO/error budgets, AsyncAPI, blameless postmortems, Tech Radar, RACI. This assessment scores those explicitly — 25 practices, 5 axis-groups, weighted average.

Overall: ≈ 3.0 / 10. Honest world-class score. Strong axes are Group C — Information Delivery (≈ 4.66 / 10, carried by Diátaxis, docs-as-code, templates) and Group B — Architecture & Modeling (≈ 4.04, carried by API contract-first and C4/PlantUML). Weakest axis is Group D — Reliability & Operations (≈ 0.80 / 10 — no SLO, no error budgets, no runbook template, no blameless postmortems, no risk register, no capacity planning). Second-weakest is Group E — Process & Governance (≈ 1.76 — no SECURITY.md, no catalog YAML, no llms.txt, no stakeholder map, no RACI).

Why the score is much lower than the Tech Docs v2 audit (4.6 / 10). v2 measured docs-ops practices the project actually invests in (ADR, RFC, Diátaxis, OpenAPI). This SA Maturity assessment includes the SA-artefact ladder Tech Docs v2 didn't measure (Design Docs, postmortems, SLO, AsyncAPI, DDD, stakeholder map, risk register, capacity, RACI). Those score near zero today — and that is the honest world-class picture. The target trajectory (TO-BE ≈ 7.5 / 10) is achievable through the practices.html roadmap; this is the baseline from which improvement is measured.

Hiring lens. Persistent strengths (32 ADRs with lifecycle, 6 RFCs including RFC 0003 authoring model, Diátaxis-pure public tree, OpenAPI drift-gate, ADR 0029 DB documentation contract, page-history deployed everywhere) read as a Senior SA signal of structural thinking across multiple knowledge layers. The wider 25-practice rubric exposes the SA-artefact gap clearly: this repo is excellent at decisions, average at delivery, and weak at reliability and governance. None of the gaps require a rewrite — every one is on the practices.html roadmap.

Scope and methodology

Scope

This assessment covers the full Systems-Analysis artefact ladder — what a Senior SA produces, owns, and evolves in a world-class engineering organization. End-user UX of the rendered portal remains out of scope (covered by the DX stream); pure SRE engineering metrics (DORA 4 key metrics, RED dashboards) remain out of scope (covered by Project Maturity). This is the SA-specific lens that Tech Docs v2 and Project Maturity do not cover end-to-end.

Methodology

Every one of the 25 practices is scored 1–10 against an industry benchmark with a public URL anchor. Each AS-IS row carries a confirmed / partial / not-confirmed justification stamp and cites a file:line, a directory/, or explicit «does not exist». A row with no public benchmark URL is ineligible for credit. Severity scale used in the gap tables: SEV-1 baseline practice missing or broken; SEV-2 2023+ industry-standard practice absent or out-of-date; SEV-3 2025–2026 frontier practice absent; SEV-4 polish / tech debt without immediate harm.

For SA practices, machine-readable formats are required for «confirmed» credit. A HTML description of a service catalog is partial — only catalog-info.yaml per Backstage descriptor counts as «confirmed». Same applies to asyncapi.yaml, tech-radar.json, llms.txt, glossary/terms.yaml, and risk-register.yaml.

Files and directories read for this assessment: Makefile, .pre-commit-config.yaml, .github/workflows/*.yml, pyproject.toml, README.md, CONTRIBUTING.md, SECURITY.md (does not exist), .well-known/security.txt (does not exist), llms.txt (does not exist), tech-radar.json (does not exist), services/portal/internal/governance/adr/ (32 ADRs incl. 0001, 0020, 0024, 0025, 0026, 0029), services/portal/internal/governance/rfc/ (6 RFCs incl. 0003, 0004), services/portal/internal/analysis/practices.html, services/portal/internal/analysis/practices/ (7 principle pages), services/portal/internal/analysis/methodology.html, services/portal/internal/analysis/system-design.html, services/portal/internal/catalog/{api,portal,monitoring}/ (HTML only — no YAML), services/portal/internal/sre/{runbooks,postmortems}/, services/portal/internal/handbook/, services/portal/public/{tutorials,how-to,reference,explanation}/, services/portal/public/reference/api/openapi-baseline.json, alembic/versions/, scripts/openapi_governance.py, scripts/validate_docs_*.py, scripts/ensure_docs_page_history.py.

Scoring summary

Overall narrative

Weighted score ≈ 3.0 / 10. The project has built a strong decision layer (ADR/RFC, OpenAPI drift-gate, Diátaxis-pure tree, docs-as-code, page-history) — that's the carry. The weakness is the SA-artefact ladder: Design Docs, postmortems, runbooks, SLO, risk register, capacity, stakeholder map, RACI, SECURITY, catalog YAML, llms.txt. These are not «nice to have» — they are the artefacts a Google-level architect expects to see on day one.

The target trajectory TO-BE ≈ 7.5 / 10 is achievable in 12 months through the practices.html roadmap (group-by-group). Quick wins (SECURITY.md, llms.txt, glossary YAML, Vale, catalog YAML, runbook template, postmortem template) push score to ≈ 4.5 in 30 days. The harder lift is Group D — SLO definition, capacity planning, risk register — those require a quarterly cadence of practice, not just one-time files.
