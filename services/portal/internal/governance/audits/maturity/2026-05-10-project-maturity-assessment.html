Scope and methodology

Scope

This assessment covers the full repository at /Users/ivan_boyarkin/Desktop/projects/study_app, spanning the API service (app/), CI/CD pipeline (.github/workflows/), observability stack (ops/), database layer (alembic/), test suite (tests/), and governance documentation (services/portal/internal/governance/). The documentation experience of the portal itself is out of scope — it is covered by the DX Assessment stream.

The 30 practices are grouped into 6 weighted axes: Engineering Fundamentals (15%), CI/CD & Delivery (15%), Observability & SRE (15%), Security & Compliance (15%), Architectural Governance (20%), Operations & Team (20%).

Methodology

Each practice is scored 1–10 by mapping observed repository evidence against world-class reference standards. Where a direct artifact exists (script, CI job, config file, ADR) the row is marked confirmed. Where the score relies on structural inspection or absence evidence, it is marked not confirmed. Scores are not self-reported — every cell cites a concrete file path or ADR.

Files read for this assessment: Makefile, .github/workflows/ci.yml, pyproject.toml, .pre-commit-config.yaml, requirements.txt, CONTRIBUTING.md, scripts/container_entrypoint.sh, ops/prometheus/rules/study_app_slo.yml, ops/grafana/, ops/filebeat/, alembic/versions/, services/portal/internal/governance/adr/ (ADR 0001–0030), services/portal/internal/governance/rfc/ (RFC 0001–0005), services/portal/internal/sre/.

Scoring summary

Narrative overall

Overall: ≈ 5.2 / 10. The repository exhibits an unusual split: two axes are genuinely world-class (Architectural Governance at 7.6 and Engineering Fundamentals at 8.2), while two axes are critically weak (Security & Compliance at 2.0 and Operations & Team at 3.6). This combination is characteristic of a technically disciplined solo project that has invested heavily in decision-making hygiene but has not yet confronted production-grade operational and security requirements.

The ADR/RFC governance, OpenAPI contract enforcement, and linting/coverage gates are at or above the level of mature engineering teams at GitLab, Stripe, and Spotify — a genuine strength that provides a strong foundation for future scaling. The CI/CD pipeline produces a publishable artefact but stops at the registry boundary, leaving the entire deployment, rollback, and release process manual and undocumented.

To move toward top decile: the path runs through Security (add SECURITY.md, incident response runbook, encryption at rest) and Delivery (add a staging deploy step, rollback runbook, semantic-release automation). Observability gaps (distributed tracing, alert routing) are the next layer. Infrastructure as Code and feature flags are longer-term investments.
