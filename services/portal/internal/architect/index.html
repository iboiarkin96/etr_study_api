Architect portal

What this portal is for

This portal is the architecture home of the project. It answers one question:

"What does a top-tier startup architect actually do, week-to-week, that distinguishes them from a senior engineer?"

The answer is 25 practices, grouped into 5 themes — strategy & alignment, structure & boundaries, quality attributes, evolution & governance, and craft. Each practice is described on its own page with definition, why it matters, how Stripe / Shopify / GitLab / Cloudflare / Datadog and friends apply it, the anatomy, common pitfalls, and a quality bar.

Start here

  • Practices radar (25) — the visual panorama: AS-IS vs TO-BE scores on all 25 axes plus a card-per-practice summary, with direct entry into any of the 25 detail pages.

The 5 groups

  • Group A · Strategy & alignment — architecture vision, technology radar, build-vs-buy, north-star metric translation, stakeholder map.
  • Group B · Structure & boundaries — bounded contexts, service decomposition, API contract design, data ownership, integration patterns.
  • Group C · Quality attributes — non-functional requirements, reliability targets, performance budgets, security posture, cost & FinOps.
  • Group D · Evolution & governance — ADRs, RFCs, fitness functions, deprecation playbooks, architectural reviews.
  • Group E · Craft — design docs, C4 / PlantUML diagrams, technical writing, mentoring, deep code reviews.

How this set was chosen

The 25 practices were selected from the public architecture writing of teams that publish how they design systems (Stripe, Shopify, GitLab, Cloudflare, Datadog, HashiCorp, Linear, Figma, Vercel, Netflix, Google) cross-referenced with the canonical literature (Fowler, Richards & Ford, Newman, Vernon, Evans). The bar for inclusion was: a practice has to be (a) attributable to a named source or originator, (b) verifiable in production at one or more top-tier startups, and (c) something an individual architect can practice — not just an org-level policy.

The AS-IS / TO-BE scores on each practice are an honest, conservative assessment of where this project stands today and where the maturity audit (May 2026) target sits.
