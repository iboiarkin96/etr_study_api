Dual-track Agile — discovery and delivery in parallel

Engineers build validated work while PMs stay two sprints ahead discovering what to build next.

TL;DR

Dual-track Agile (Marty Cagan / Jeff Patton) runs two concurrent tracks: a Discovery track where PMs and designers validate solutions before engineering begins, and a Delivery track where engineers build pre-validated work. The rule is strict: only work that passed discovery enters the delivery sprint. Engineers are never idle waiting for requirements; PMs are never throwing unvalidated work over a wall.

What it is

Dual-track Agile was articulated by Marty Cagan (SVPG) and Jeff Patton as a structured way to prevent the two most common wastes in product development: engineers waiting for requirements and engineers building unvalidated features.

The Discovery track is owned by PM and design. It runs continuously — before, during, and after delivery sprints. Discovery work includes: customer interviews, prototyping, usability testing, assumption testing, and writing the product brief and acceptance criteria. Work exits the discovery track only when the team is confident (≥70%) that building it will produce the expected customer outcome.

The Delivery track is owned by engineering. It receives only work that has cleared the discovery gate. Engineering's job is to build what was validated, not to validate while building. This keeps the delivery track predictable and gives engineers full context on the why before they write a line of code.

Why we need it

In a single-track process, discovery and delivery compete for the same team's time. When delivery pressure peaks (before a release), discovery stops. When discovery runs ahead (at a project start), engineers are idle. Dual-track eliminates both by separating the two activities into independent tracks that run in parallel.

The deeper benefit is a cultural shift: engineers become collaborators in the discovery process (attending user research, contributing to prototypes) without being blocked by it. They see the evidence behind every story before they pick it up, which dramatically reduces mid-sprint questions and scope changes.

How others do it

SVPG (Marty Cagan)

Cagan's framework defines four big risks that discovery must address before delivery: value risk (will customers buy it?), usability risk (can customers use it?), feasibility risk (can we build it?), and business viability risk (will it work for the business?). All four must be mitigated in the discovery track before the delivery sprint begins.

What we take: The four risk categories make discovery tractable. For each feature in the discovery backlog, explicitly score the four risks — this prevents the team from doing only usability testing and ignoring feasibility until mid-sprint.

Source: SVPG — Dual-track Agile

Spotify

Spotify's squad model embeds a designer in every squad who maintains a "discovery backlog" separate from the delivery sprint backlog. The designer runs two to three usability sessions per sprint while the squad ships the previous sprint's validated work. The PM reviews both backlogs weekly.

What we take: A dedicated discovery backlog — separate from the Jira sprint board — normalises the existence of two parallel workstreams. Teams with a single backlog always deprioritise discovery when delivery pressure rises.

Source: Spotify Engineering Blog

Anatomy

Discovery gate criteria

Written checklist: customer tested? Confidence ≥70%? Brief complete? AC written? All four SVPG risks addressed?

Discovery backlog

Separate from the delivery sprint board. PM-owned. Contains ideas under investigation, not committed work.

Delivery backlog

Contains only work that has cleared the discovery gate. Engineering picks from here during sprint planning.

Discovery velocity

The team tracks how many items clear the discovery gate per sprint. If fewer than 2 clear per sprint, delivery will starve.

Quality bar — what good looks like

  • ○

    Separate discovery and delivery backlogs maintained

    Not a single Jira board with a "Discovery" label.

  • ○

    Discovery gate criteria written and enforced

    PM cannot bypass the gate without a written escalation.

  • ○

    Delivery backlog has ≥2 sprints of validated work at all times

    PM is responsible for maintaining this buffer. Reviewed weekly.
