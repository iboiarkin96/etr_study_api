Site Reliability Engineering

What this portal is for

This portal is the operational home of the service. It answers three practical questions:

  1. Is the service healthy right now? If not, where is the problem and how bad is it?
  2. What do I do during an incident? Who declares it, who fixes it, who tells users.
  3. What did we learn after the incident? Honest, blameless write-ups so the same outage does not happen twice.

The structure follows the Google SRE Book chapter spine and the project rules in RFC 0004.

How to use this portal

Read in this order if SRE is new to you:

  1. SLOs & error budget — what "healthy" actually means in numbers.
  2. Monitoring — the metrics and dashboards that prove it.
  3. Logging — the structured story of every request.
  4. Observability — how metrics, logs, traces, and health checks add up to a full picture.
  5. On-call — who reacts when an alert fires.
  6. Incident response — the playbook for a live outage.
  7. Debugging — diagnostic patterns for production bugs.
  8. Postmortems — the blameless write-up after an incident is over.
  9. Runbooks — short, per-alert recipes you follow at 3 a.m.

Reliability foundations

  • SLOs & error budget — service-level objectives, indicators, and the error budget contract per ADR 0011.
  • Monitoring — metrics and dashboards per ADR 0009.
  • Logging — structured logs and local Elasticsearch per ADR 0023.
  • Observability — the end-to-end view that connects logs, metrics, traces, and health endpoints.

Operations

  • On-call — alert routing, escalation, and what a shift looks like in a solo project.
  • Incident response — the declare → mitigate → communicate → resolve loop.
  • Debugging — a diagnostic playbook for production issues.

Postmortems and runbooks

  • Postmortems — blameless write-ups; template for new incidents.
  • Runbooks — per-alert procedures (CI failures, security checks, observability scrape, error budget burn, and more).

Glossary in one paragraph

An SLI is a measurement (for example, the share of HTTP 200 responses). An SLO is the target we promise ourselves (for example, "99.5% of requests succeed over 30 days"). An SLA is what we promise to a paying customer; this project does not have one yet. The error budget is what is left of the SLO — if we promise 99.5% and we are at 99.7%, we still have budget to spend on risky changes. A runbook is a short procedure for one specific alert. A postmortem is the honest story of an incident, written without blame, with concrete action items.
