The 3-minute mental model

Read this section out loud. If it takes under three minutes and a newcomer can answer «where do I edit so a bug doesn't reach prod?» — the model works. Everything else on this hub is just a zoomed-in view of one of the four boxes below.

One monorepo: four services

Three sentences out loud

What I'm building

A monorepo of four services — api (FastAPI), portal (Diátaxis docs-as-code), monitoring (Prometheus / Grafana), frontend (UI Kit). The hardest is the portal: hundreds of pages across page-types, templates, and a nav-tree. The real risk in all four is the same — drift between things that have to change together.

Where things live

Every portal page has a shape: six data-* attributes on <body>, one of ten kit templates under assets_v2/ui-kit/templates/, an entry in nav-tree-internal.json. Break the shape and the gate fires. Break a coupling — edit a template without its UI-kit twin, change a Make target without logging it here — and a different gate fires.

What keeps me safe

Three rings of gates: pre-commit (twelve local hooks plus ruff/mypy), make docs-check (the drift gate — regenerates autogen, fails on any uncommitted delta), CI matrix (same verify on a clean clone, plus deploy). Two of the five coupling rules are hard pre-commit gates already; three are memory rules with a documented home on the gates map.

Five rules

Every rule is enforceable. A deviation requires an ADR.

  1. 01 · Where the page lives · ADR-0032 D1

    Placement is service or topic, not quadrant

    A page about a service goes under services/<svc>/; a cross-cutting page goes under handbook/ with data-topic. The Diátaxis quadrant is metadata (data-page-type), not a folder. The routing decision tree walks you through the six questions of ADR-0032 D1–D6.

    Open the decision tree →
  2. 02 · What every page declares · ADR-0032 D7

    Six data-* attributes on <body>

    data-page-type, data-service, data-role, data-topic, data-lifecycle, data-updated. Without them the page silently falls out of catalog views. tools/docs/validate_docs_html.py hard-enforces the contract at every commit. Allowed values and per-type examples on the page-type contract.

    Open the contract →
  3. 03 · Before every commit · drift gate

    docs-fix then docs-check

    Run make -C services/portal docs-fix && make -C services/portal docs-check. The drift gate fails on any uncommitted autogen delta. Pre-commit hooks alone do not catch autogen drift (catalog, search index, maintainers). For service pages add make verify on top.

    Open the gates map →
  4. 04 · How you start · handbook/sa/templates/

    Every new page starts from a template

    Copy from handbook/sa/templates/ — 16 templates, one per artefact (ADR, RFC, runbook, postmortem, how-to, tutorial, audit, etc.). Structure is contract, not self-expression. The master five-step workflow at handbook/sa/authoring/add-a-page wraps the copy step.

    Open the templates →
  5. 05 · Close the loop · coupling

    Process changes leave a trail; coupled edits travel together

    A new Make target, pre-commit hook, CI workflow, SA template, or operations-class ADR must update the «what's new» feed in the same PR. A change to a SA template must travel with its UI-kit twin (pairs in tests/visual/pages.json) and a refreshed baseline. Both enforced by pre-commit gates at every commit.
