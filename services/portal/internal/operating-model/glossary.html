Internal · Operating model · Reference

Process vocabulary — the words this section uses

Process-side terms only — what we mean by hub, lens, drift, twin, coupling, station, feed, baseline, autogen, and the rest. Grouped by topic, not alphabet, because in a small vocabulary the topics are more useful than the letters. Product and domain terms (users, contracts, study sessions, conspectus) live in the onboarding glossary.

How to read this page

  • Each term is one row: name on the left, definition on the right. Cross-references inside a definition link to the term they name.
  • Terms are grouped by the surface they describe — hub, metadata, lifecycle, gates, coupling. Read a single group when you arrived from a specific page; read top-to-bottom when you are learning the section.
  • The four closed vocabularies from the page-type contract (data-page-type, data-service, data-role, data-lifecycle) are listed there, not here — this page is the conceptual vocabulary that surrounds them.
A

Hub & navigation

5 terms
Operating model
The set of rules, gates, and coupling-loops that govern how a change reaches production. This section is the visible surface of the operating model; per ADR 0035 it lives at services/portal/internal/operating-model/.
Hub
A section's landing page — the one entry point readers reach first. The operating-model hub is operating-model/index.html: five rules, three quick-paths, and the last ten feed entries. Hubs synthesise rather than re-explain.
Router page
A page whose primary job is to send the reader to another page — the routing decision tree, the quality gates map. Router pages are short and dense; deep narrative lives in the handbook page they point to.
Quick-path
One of the three large CTA tiles on the operating-model hub. Each names a common authoring session («adding a page», «a gate failed», «changing the process itself») and links to the page that answers it.
Station
One of the ten ordered steps on lifecycle of a change. A station has a fixed input, a fixed output, and an explicit list of gates that fire at it. «What station am I at?» is the most useful question during a long change.
B

Diátaxis & metadata

5 terms
Quadrant
One of the four Diátaxis lenses: tutorial, how-to, reference, explanation. The quadrant is metadata (data-page-type), never a folder — per ADR-0032 D3. Theory at Diátaxis.
Lens
A view that slices the page set on one axis without moving pages on disk. The portal has three: quadrant (by-quadrant/), service (by-service/), topic (by-topic/). Role hubs are a fourth conceptual lens that aggregates via tags.
Canon
The «correct shape» for a class of pages. The SA handbook documents canons (page-skeleton, voice, document-types); the design baseline enforces them; the templates instantiate them. «Off-canon» pages fail the design gate.
Page skeleton
The five required HTML slots every page must satisfy (page-head, lede, breadcrumbs, sidebar mount, topbar). Documented at the page skeleton; enforced by docs-design-check.
Lede
The first paragraph after the h1 — one short stretch of prose that promises what the page will do for the reader. Required by the page skeleton; rendered as <p class="lede"> or <p class="home-hero__tagline">.
C

Lifecycle & freshness

4 terms
Lifecycle state
One of draft / review / published / deprecated; declared via data-lifecycle. Aligned with the ADR-0018 vocabulary; controls how a page surfaces in catalog views.
Recency
«How fresh is this page?» — measured as days since data-updated. Drives catalog/recent.html and the freshness audit.
Freshness
The contract that says published pages must be re-read within an SLA per document type. Documented at the freshness contract. Stale pages fail the audit; the freshness state is a separate signal from recency (you can be recently updated and still deprecated).
Maintainer chip
The data-maintainer-ids body attribute that powers the «edited by» block at the page foot. Auto-injected by the docs-maintainers-fix pre-commit hook if you forget; the design gate fails the commit otherwise.
D

Gates & drift

6 terms
Gate
A check that can fail the commit, push, or merge. Three environments — pre-commit (local), Make targets (local + CI), CI workflows (cloud) — inventoried on the quality gates map.
Hook
A specific pre-commit gate, configured in .pre-commit-config.yaml. Twelve are stock-or-Python; eight are repo-local Python under tools/.
Drift
A difference between (a) what an autogen source declares and (b) what the rendered output on disk shows. Examples: catalog-info.yaml vs the rendered service overview; the marker block in a markdown vs the companion HTML.
Drift gate
Specifically make docs-check: runs docs-fix, diffs git diff HEAD before vs after, fails if anything moved. The single gate that catches «I forgot to run docs-fix».
Autogen
«Autogenerated». The portal has many autogen outputs — catalog views, pdoc tree, Pagefind index, service overview HTML, the README directory tree. Authors must not hand-edit autogen outputs; docs-fix writes them.
Autogen source
The input that docs-fix reads to produce an autogen output. Examples: catalog-info.yaml, marker blocks in *.md, *.puml, pdoc-eligible Python under services/api/app/. Edit the source, run docs-fix, stage both.
E

Coupling

6 terms
Coupling
A structural rule «edit X ⇒ also edit Y» that a single author cannot reliably remember. Coupling rules are the unique surface of the operating model — they exist precisely because the gates need to do the remembering. Inventoried on the quality gates map § Coupling rules.
Coupling rule
One row in the coupling inventory. Five columns: trigger path, must-also-change, why, enforced-by, state (memory rule vs hard gate). Adding a new rule means one row in the inventory and one line in the enforcer.
Twin
A pair of pages that share a visual baseline — currently the SA template under handbook/sa/templates/<X>.html and its UI-kit counterpart under portal/ui-kit/pages/templates/<Y>.html. Pairs are declared in tests/visual/pages.json. The twin gate (check_template_twins.py) refuses one-sided commits.
Baseline
The committed reference image that a visual-regression test compares against. Refreshed via make -C services/portal visual-test-update when the visual changed intentionally; lives under tests/visual/baselines/.
Meta-change
A change that touches the process surface itself — a new Make target, pre-commit hook, CI workflow, SA template, an operations-class ADR, or a script under tools/docs/. Every meta-change must travel with a feed entry; check_meta_changes_logged.py enforces it.
Feed
Short for «what's new in how we work feed» — the curated meta-change log at the what's-new feed. Hand-authored, four columns per entry (date · what · why · what changes for you), enforced by the meta-changes-logged pre-commit hook at every commit.
