Decision RACI — who decides, who does, who is consulted

A decision matrix that makes authority explicit — preventing the two most common decision failures: no one decides, or everyone decides.

TL;DR

A Decision RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) is a matrix that maps recurring product decisions to clear roles. Accountable is the single person who owns the outcome (only one per decision). Responsible is the person who does the work. Consulted are those whose input is required before deciding. Informed are those who must be notified after deciding. A Decision RACI is not a task list — it covers recurring decision types (scope changes, pricing, launch go/no-go) so that when a decision moment arrives, the process is pre-agreed.

What it is

A Decision RACI is a matrix that maps recurring product decision types to clear roles using four categories:

  • Responsible (R): The person who does the work — gathers information, writes the proposal, presents the analysis. May be a different person than the Accountable.
  • Accountable (A): The single person who owns the outcome of the decision. Only one A per decision type. If no one is accountable, no one is. If two people are accountable, the decision will be delayed or made inconsistently.
  • Consulted (C): People whose input is required before the decision is made. Their concerns must be heard and addressed, but they do not have veto power.
  • Informed (I): People who are notified of the decision after it is made. They are not consulted — being informed is not an opportunity to reverse the decision.

A Decision RACI covers recurring decision types — scope changes, pricing, launch go/no-go, technical architecture, UX direction — so that when the decision moment arrives, the process is already agreed and the team does not need to negotiate authority in the moment.

Why we need it

The two most common decision failures in product teams are: no one decides (because no one has clear authority) and everyone decides (because authority is unclear, so every stakeholder feels entitled to weigh in). Both failures produce delays and resentment. A RACI makes authority explicit and non-negotiable: if you are not the Accountable, you can be Consulted, but you do not block the decision.

The RACI also manages the "consulted" workload fairly. When consulting is ad hoc — the PM asks whoever is nearby — the same people are consulted repeatedly while others are never engaged. A RACI makes consultation explicit and predictable, which reduces the PM's social tax of remembering who to ask and the frustration of stakeholders who feel bypassed.

How others do it

Amazon (single-threaded owner)

Amazon uses the concept of a "single-threaded owner" (STO) for every significant product decision: one named person who is wholly responsible for the outcome and whose primary job function is that initiative. The STO is the Accountable in RACI terms. Amazon's culture is explicit: an initiative without an STO will not succeed because every competing priority will erode it. The STO removes ambiguity by design.

What we take: The "single-threaded" principle is the most important element of RACI. For every significant product decision, there is exactly one person who will be measured on the outcome. Make that person explicit in the RACI — and make sure they know they are the STO, not a consulted party.

Source: Amazon Leadership Principles

Spotify

Spotify squads maintain a "decision log" — a living document that records significant product decisions with the Accountable, date, options considered, and rationale. The decision log is linked to the RACI: every row in the RACI that lists the squad as Accountable generates a decision log entry when a decision is made. The log is shared with all squads and referenced in postmortems to trace which decisions led to which outcomes.

What we take: A decision log paired with a RACI creates an audit trail. When a decision produces a bad outcome, the postmortem can trace back to who was Accountable, what options were considered, and why the chosen option was selected. This closes the learning loop without requiring memory or blame.

Source: Spotify Engineering Blog

Anatomy

Decision types (rows)

List 8–15 recurring decision types: scope changes, tech architecture, UX direction, launch go/no-go, pricing, hiring, partnership. Not one-off decisions — recurring patterns.

One A per row (rule)

Every row has exactly one Accountable. If two roles both say A, the RACI is wrong. Resolve the ambiguity before the next decision moment, not during it.

Consulted vs. Informed distinction

C means "their input shapes the decision." I means "they are told after." Misclassifying I as C creates unnecessary consultation overhead and sets false expectations.

Escalation path

What happens if the Accountable cannot make the decision? The RACI must include the escalation path (who the Accountable escalates to) and the escalation trigger (what condition requires escalation).

Quarterly review

Review the RACI at each quarterly retrospective. Decision authority changes as the team grows, roles change, and product complexity evolves. A stale RACI is worse than no RACI.
