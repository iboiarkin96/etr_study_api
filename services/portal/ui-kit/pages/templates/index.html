Page templates

Nine end-to-end specimens that compose the kit into ready-to-clone pages. Every portal page derives its chrome and rhythm from one of these — clone the file whole, swap the copy, leave the kit alone. New templates start from the screen-spec template and follow the CSS-architecture canon (no per-page CSS; promote shared styles to the kit).

Landing · top-of-funnel surfaces

Front-door pages for a portal or a section. Generous hero, opinionated tile grid, no on-this-page TOC. Used when the reader has just arrived and you want to give them a map, not a manual.

  1. Landing · Portal

    Portal landing

    The front door of an entire portal. WebGL hero, four pillar tiles, live tickers; no sidebar TOC. The richest specimen — clone this when you build a brand-new portal home.

    Open template→
  2. Landing · Section

    Section landing

    One step inside the portal — a category index (analysis / governance / SRE). Compact hero, feature-card grid, lightweight chrome. The fallback for any «section's home page».

    Open template→

Diátaxis · doc page shapes

Four Diátaxis quadrants plus the generic article fallback. Every doc-page in the portal picks one of these specimens as its starting shape; the chosen data-page-type is what the portal validators read.

  1. Diátaxis · Article

    Doc · article

    Generic prose article — pick this when none of the four Diátaxis quadrants fits cleanly. Right-side TOC, footer history, no procedural rails.

    Open template→
  2. Diátaxis · Tutorial

    Doc · tutorial

    First-time-through learning. Numbered steps, success checkpoints, a single «Next step» card (no See-also). For when the reader is new to the territory.

    Open template→
  3. Diátaxis · How-to

    Doc · how-to

    Task-oriented recipe: Before-you-start · Steps · Verify · Troubleshooting · Related. Reader already knows the territory and wants to get the job done.

    Open template→
  4. Diátaxis · Reference

    Doc · reference

    Authoritative facts — endpoints, fields, schemas. Endpoint cards, field rows, tables, sticky-head, method badges. Dense but scannable; no narrative.

    Open template→
  5. Diátaxis · Explanation

    Doc · explanation

    Long-form prose — «why we use X». Concept layers, lens chips, deep-dive callouts. Light on code, heavy on rationale and trade-offs.

    Open template→

Ops · 3 a.m. surfaces

For things people read under pressure. Higher information density, lower decoration. Dense state surfaces and procedural runbooks — the reader is mid-incident and wants the next action, not a story.

  1. Ops · Cockpit

    Ops · cockpit

    Live-state dashboard. Filter chips, view switcher, tasks grid, state-strip, sparklines, escalation banner. The shape backlog and SLO pages take.

    Open template→
  2. Ops · Runbook

    Ops · runbook

    Incident response. «Do this now» block at the top, severity pill, escalation chain, interactive checklist with numbered steps. For everything in services/<svc>/runbooks/.

    Open template→

Governance · decision & review records

Doc-shapes the team uses to record decisions, reviews, and cross-cutting policy. Specialised reference surfaces — same chrome as the kit but with mandatory structural sections that audits and reviewers expect.

  1. Governance · ADR

    Doc · ADR

    Architecture Decision Record. Lifecycle-popover status pill, mandatory ≥ 2 named rejected alternatives with explicit «Rejected because…», Consequences split into Positive / Trade-offs, Compatibility & migration, Validation. For everything in governance/adr/.
