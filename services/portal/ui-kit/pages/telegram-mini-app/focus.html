Screen DNA · study theater

Amie · signature
  • The tap surface is the moment. Full-screen chrome-free — no topbar, no nav, no distractions. Card + grades + progress dots is the entire screen.
  • Card is a real flashcard. 3D flip on tap-to-reveal (550 ms, back-face pre-rotated so text reads normally); ember bloom pulses from centre; answer content staggers in half-way through the flip. Tap again → flip back.
  • Four grades, three server tags. Again · Hard · Good · Easy — each with its own tone (danger / warn / accent / success). Good collapses to hard server-side until the 4-tag ETR contract lands; distinction preserved client-side for analytics.
  • One-tap flow. Reveal → grade → next card. Session cap 20 (Duolingo-tier — anything longer degrades recall). Keyboard on desktop: Space reveal · 1–4 grade · Esc exit.
  • Pessimistic mutation. Card leaves the queue only on server-ack; no ghost cards on 500s. Reuses the same useReviewConspectus the Today swipe uses.

06 · Focus · Philosophy

Why Focus matters

Everything else in the app is reading; Focus is practice. Single-tap flow — reveal, grade, next card, repeat. No topbar, no navigation. Just card + grades + progress dots. Session cap 20 cards (Duolingo-tier — anything longer degrades recall).

The design intent is single-tap flow. If you catch yourself building anything more, delete it.
