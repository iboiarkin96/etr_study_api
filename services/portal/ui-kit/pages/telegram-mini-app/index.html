Philosophy · eight choices

Eight design choices that make this feel like a personal product, not a work tool. Each one is a deliberate pull toward the daily habit — read them as decisions we made, not defaults we accepted.

01

Warmth is a signal

Signature is ember (coral) paired with sage as the cool «rested» tone. Warm accents live in products people return to daily — Reflect, Bear, Amie, Sunsama. We chose ember because habit-building deserves a colour that greets you, not one that clocks you in.

02

Numbers breathe

Streak, milestone, session count live in a display tier — 800 weight, tabular figures, tight -0.035em tracking, warm gradient text. Numbers are the emotional pulse of a habit app; we made them big and typographically loud instead of hiding them in data-cells.

03

One living orb

The breathing orb is the single signature moment — the thing users describe to a friend without opening the app. It breathes in idle (5 s), warms when a streak is active, pulses on milestone, cools to sage when the day is done. Every screen has one; nothing else asks for a second signature.

04

States that whisper

Success / warning / danger use Anthropic-doc pastels — Tailwind-200 fills + Tailwind-700 ink. Flat, no glow, no bloom. They inform without shouting so the ember signature stays the loudest thing on screen. Calm callouts, not alarm sirens.

05

One motion character

Expressive — 240 ms spring with a 1–3 % overshoot. Not a menu of three personalities to pick from at build-time; one shipped opinion. A system that knows itself feels calmer than one that keeps its options open.

06

Mini App speaks Telegram

Cells, sections, bottom-sheets, MainButton, haptic vocabulary — every pattern comes from Telegram's iOS / Material contract. We ship warmth on top of that contract, not a webapp jammed inside a chat client.

07

The client owns theming

Every colour reads from var(--tg-theme-*). When the user flips dark / light or picks a custom Telegram theme, the Mini App follows for free. Zero shipped webfonts — SF Pro on iOS, Roboto on Android — because doubling FCP on cold LTE is not a habit-app trade-off worth making.

08

Made for thumbs

Tap targets ≥ 44 px. Sections group 2–6 rows. Every screen answers exactly one question — «what do I do right now?» — inside 2 seconds. Tables live on the portal; the Mini App never asks a thumb to do a mouse's work.
